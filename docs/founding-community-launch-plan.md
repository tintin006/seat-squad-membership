# SEAT Squad Founding Community Launch Plan

Last updated: 2026-05-05

## Goal

Open The SEAT Squad with enough substance that a first visitor can immediately understand:

- Who this is for
- Why it exists
- What to do first
- What kind of support they can expect
- Why returning next week will be worth it

The goal is not to make the community look huge. The goal is to make it feel alive, useful, specific, and cared for.

Launch implication: the first public version should center on community, welcome, prompts, orientation, and founder-led value. Do not wait for every feature.

## Who It Is For

Primary:

- Black and Brown families
- LGBTQ-inclusive families
- Neurodivergent learners and their caregivers
- Special-needs and disability-support families
- Hybrid homeschoolers
- Families leaving, supplementing, or repairing their relationship with traditional school

Secondary:

- Tutors
- Educators
- Education coaches and consultants
- Micro school and homeschool pod leaders

The positioning should be specific enough that the right families feel recognized, not broadly "parent community" generic.

## The Day 1 Promise

Suggested public promise:

> The SEAT Squad is a practical community for families and educators building flexible, culturally affirming learning lives. Bring your real questions. Leave with clearer next steps.

The first version should promise:

- Practical support
- Belonging without flattening identity
- Learning ideas families can actually use
- Honest discussion about what is and is not working
- A path into Remix Academics resources, Mixtape360, and The Crate

Do not promise:

- A giant active community yet
- 24/7 expert answers
- Fully built course/resource/tutor infrastructure
- Personalized intervention plans

## Minimum Valuable Community

Before inviting traffic, seed the community with:

1. One pinned welcome post
2. One introduction thread
3. One weekly reset thread
4. One educator/tutor listening thread
5. One orientation announcement
6. One The Crate request thread

These can be added using:

`docs/founder-launch-content.sql`

## Recommended Channel Setup

Existing channels are good enough for launch:

- General
- Homeschool Tips
- Educator Lounge
- Events & Meetups
- The Crate Showcase

Recommended future channel changes after first members arrive:

- Add `Start Here`
- Rename `Homeschool Tips` to `Learning Life Lab`
- Rename `The Crate Showcase` to `The Crate Requests`
- Add `Neurodivergent Learners`
- Add `Leaving Traditional School`

Do not add too many channels before there are members. Empty channels make a small community feel smaller.

## First Week Content Rhythm

### Day 1

Post:

- Welcome to The SEAT Squad
- Introduce yourself
- Weekly reset

Action:

- Invite 5-10 trusted founding testers personally, not through broad public ads.

### Day 2

Post:

- "What is one learning friction point you wish more people understood?"

Action:

- Reply to every intro with a thoughtful follow-up question.

### Day 3

Post:

- "What would make next week easier: structure, curriculum, confidence, accountability, or support?"

Action:

- Capture repeated needs into a private founder notes doc.

### Day 4

Post:

- Educator/tutor listening prompt.

Action:

- Ask one educator or tutor to share a useful mini-framework.

### Day 5

Post:

- The Crate request thread.

Action:

- Choose one member request and turn it into a simple resource.

### Day 6

Post:

- "Weekend check-in: what are you trying, avoiding, or rethinking?"

Action:

- Invite members to vote on the first orientation time.

### Day 7

Post:

- "What should we build first?"

Action:

- Summarize the week in one founder post.

## Launch Content Principles

Good SEAT Squad posts should be:

- Specific
- Warm
- Practical
- Identity-aware
- Non-performative
- Easy to answer in under two minutes

Avoid:

- Generic parenting advice
- Huge abstract questions
- "Tell us your whole story" prompts
- Posts that make parents feel behind
- Over-polished corporate language
- Content that treats diverse families as one interchangeable group

## First Founder Invite Script

Use direct invites first:

> I am opening the first version of The SEAT Squad, a Remix Academics community for families and educators building more flexible, culturally affirming learning lives. It is intentionally small right now. I would love for you to come in as a founding member, introduce yourself, and tell me what kind of support would actually be useful to your family or work.

For educators/tutors:

> I am opening the first version of The SEAT Squad and want educators/tutors in early so the family support is practical, not generic. I would love your eyes on the community and your thoughts on what families tend to need before things reach crisis mode.

## Start Here MVP

The next app feature should probably be a lightweight `/start-here` page or dashboard panel with:

- What SEAT Squad is
- What to do first
- The five community norms
- Links to intro thread, weekly reset, and Crate request thread
- "Ask for help" prompt

This gives new members a soft landing even when the community is still small.

## Community Norms Draft

1. Be specific enough to be useful.
2. Respect the family behind the question.
3. Do not flatten identity or assume one right path.
4. Share what worked for you without prescribing it for everyone.
5. Protect kids' privacy. No identifying details without consent.

## Success Criteria For First 30 Days

Useful signals:

- 20-50 founding members
- At least 10 intros
- At least 5 substantive comments
- At least 5 resource requests
- At least 1 educator/tutor contribution
- First orientation held or scheduled
- Clear top 3 pain points identified

The most important early metric is not member count. It is whether people reveal real needs that Remix Academics can serve.
